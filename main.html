From Wikipedia: Reversi is an abstract strategy board game for two players, played on an 8×8 uncheckered board. It was invented in 1883. The page goes on to explain in 1971 it was patented by Goro Hasegawa with slight variations under the more recognizable name Othello. He decided to name the conflict between black and white chips after main characters Moor Othello and Iago from the Shakespearean play Othello, the Moor of Venice.

The game is played in all eight directions. The first two moves by each player are played in the center four squares. Every turn after, a chip must be placed such that an opposing chip (or better yet a line of them) is/are surrounded on either side by two of the placer's chips without gaps inbetween. These surrounded chips are then captured and flipped. A turn must be taken such that at least one opposing chip is captured. If no such move exists for a player, their turn is skipped.

The game ends when all spaces are occupied. The chips are then counted and the player with the most is victorious.

If helper mode is enabled, all legal moves will be displayed as orange squares on the board. Reversi is a solved game. If both players play perfectly, then the result will always be a tie. The opponent included in the Single Player game mode will not play a perfect game, it will only play the move that leads to the most captures on the current board.

Every new game will generate a game code. To pick up where you left off, just enter your game code to the left and click to retrieve your game.
