Reversi

From Wikipedia: Reversi is an abstract strategy board game for two players, played on an 8×8 uncheckered board. It was invented in 1883. In 1971 it was patented by Goro Hasegawa with slight variations under the more recognizable name Othello.

The game is played in all eight directions. If helper is enabled, all legal moves will be displayed as orange squares on the board. The first two moves are played in the center four squares. Every turn after, a chip must be placed such that an opposing chip (or better yet a line of them) are surrounded on either side by one of the placer's chips. These surrrounded chips are then captured and flipped. A turn must be taken such that at least on opposing chip is captured. If no such move exists for a player, their turn is skipped.

The game ends when all spaces are occupied. The chips are then counted and the player with the most is victorious.
